Game Concepts

A cartoon action adventure in the “rubber hose” style of classic animation. Filled with wacky characters, intense battles, and ruthlessly unforgiving (and therefore extremely satisfying) gameplay.

Paradigm Tilt Concept:
Not a direct rip of “Cuphead” the game or any of its characters, but an original story using the same aesthetic and game structure. The characters should be designed in coordination with, and illustrated by, the creators of Cuphead—so that this endeavor is a pseudo-licensed iteration and not a ripoff.
Advantages
  • Fun,cartoony aesthetic is approachable and lighthearted
  • Bright colors and upbeat music are immediately engaging
  • Animation style blends well with playfield art and toys
  • Game mechanics can translate to pinball in fun and challenging ways
Disadvantages
  • Cuphead is licensed by Netflix for a new TV show, so creators at MDHR may be busy or under contract to not do other animation
  • Creating new characters in the aesthetic may be received poorly, even if they are appropriately licensed

A tonge-in-cheek adventure set on a barren, post-apocalyptic planet, this expansive game series offers rich lore, colorful characters, and plenty of antics as dozens of factions vie for power, prestige, and the promise of wealth hidden in secret “Vaults” around the world. The main series are lightly-storied affairs that offer a great backdrop but little narrative. Telltale’s spectacular episodic series Tales from the Borderlands focuses on comedy, character, and heart.

Paradigm Tilt Concept:
A direct license of Borderlands or an original story in a sci-fi desolate wasteland, focused on adventure and exploration (and guns).
Advantages
  • Broad open-world provides huge opportunites for original stories
  • Cell-shaded art style would pair well with DMD and playfield art
  • Game mechanics nearly identical to Mass Effect—great for pinball modes!
Disadvantages
  • Borderlands is a very popular and active IP, licensing could be expensive
  • Post-apocalypse is a well-tred pinball genre, including many knock-offs
  • Backstory and character development may be more difficult
A growing genre of indie games is the rogue-like dungeon crawler, specifically retro style action games that revive the nostalgia of the 8-bit era. Procedurally generated dungeons, endless item quests and upgrades, classic enemies, and upbeat chiptunes are all hallmarks of this popular genre.
Paradigm Tilt Concept:
A pinball adventure mirroring an onscreen 8-bit game—essentially two games in one—using procedurally-generated dungeouns to create modes completed via the pinball table. A well-scripted story provides a through current while player choices impact the direction.
Advantages
  • Procedural generation could be a new approach to pinball modes
  • 8-bit art and music are retro, popular, and in the DNA of pinball
  • ittle or no license restrictions on the original IP
  • Dungeons a potentially strong avenue for character and storytelling
Disadvantages
  • Substantial overhead to implement a procedural dungeon crawler game in addition to a pinball game
  • 8-bit may get tiresome to players, not ideal for long play sessions
The moon has broken into seven pieces, and the fragments are colliding and spreading at an exponential rate. Within two years, Earth will be carpeted under a hailstorm of meteors, setting the atmosphere on fire and incinerating the planet. Humanity must use what time it has left to create a sustainable population on the International Space Station that can survive for thousands of years—or else humanity will go extinct.
Paradigm Tilt Concept:
A space-action game akin to Apollo 13 but with the scale and stakes of Seveneves, this multi-phase game will cover the epic journey of the I.S.S. from small science outpost to humankind’s last hope.
Advantages
  • Local author, strongest possibility of licensing agreement
  • Space is a very popular and workable concept for pinball games
  • Epic story and high stakes drama without existing visual media
  • Huge potential for orbital/gravity-dynamic-based mechs
Disadvantages
  • pace toys and complex mechanics could be costly and error-prone
  • Low name recognition and the title isn’t intuitive to the concept
